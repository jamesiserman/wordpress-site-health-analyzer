POUR Principles — What Tessera Checks

Perceivable

Alt text for images, captions for media, sufficient color contrast, text alternatives

Operable

Keyboard navigation, focus indicators, skip links, no time traps, clear focus order

Understandable

Readable content, predictable navigation, input labels, error identification

Robust

Valid HTML, ARIA attributes, compatible with screen readers and assistive tech

Most Common Issues We Find

  • 🖼️

    Missing Alt Text (86% of sites)

    Images without descriptive alt attributes are invisible to screen reader users

  • 🔤

    Low Color Contrast (83% of sites)

    Text that doesn't meet 4.5:1 contrast ratio is unreadable for low-vision users

  • 📑

    Broken Heading Hierarchy (58% of sites)

    Skipped heading levels (H1 to H3) confuse screen reader navigation

  • 🏷️

    Missing Form Labels (54% of sites)

    Unlabeled inputs prevent screen reader users from completing forms

Legal Landscape

United States

ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)

Web accessibility lawsuits exceeded 4,000 in 2023. Courts increasingly rule that websites are "places of public accommodation."

European Union

European Accessibility Act (EAA)

Effective June 2025. Requires digital products and services to meet accessibility standards. Covers e-commerce, banking, transport.

United Kingdom

Equality Act 2010

Service providers must make reasonable adjustments for disabled users. Non-compliant websites face legal action.

Canada

AODA (Ontario) / ACA

Mandatory WCAG AA compliance for organizations with 50+ employees. Federal Accessible Canada Act expanding coverage.
