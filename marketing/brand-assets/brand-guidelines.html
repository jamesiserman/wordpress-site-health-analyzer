Logo

The Tessera mark is composed of three interlocking hexagonal tiles with cobalt accent circles at their intersections, symbolizing compliance standards that fit together.

Tessera
Primary (light backgrounds)
Tessera
Reversed (dark backgrounds)

Clear Space & Minimum Size

Maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of one cobalt circle (the "dot"). The logo should never be displayed smaller than 24px in height for the mark alone or 120px wide for the full lockup.

Logo Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • Use the provided SVG files for all digital applications
  • Maintain the aspect ratio when scaling
  • Use the dark variant on backgrounds darker than #4a4a4a
  • Pair the mark with the "Tessera" wordmark in Inter 700
  • Ensure sufficient contrast against the background
  • Keep the clear space free of other elements

Don't

  • Stretch, rotate, or distort the logo
  • Change the stroke color of the hexagonal tiles
  • Replace the cobalt circles with a different color
  • Add effects like drop shadows or glows to the mark
  • Place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
  • Rearrange the mark and wordmark relationship

Usage Guidelines

General principles for applying the Tessera brand consistently.

Color Usage

  • Navy is the primary text and background color for headers and footers.
  • Cobalt is the primary action color: links, buttons, interactive highlights.
  • Limestone is for secondary text, captions, and metadata.
  • Emerald / Amber / Crimson are strictly for status: pass, warning, fail. Never use them decoratively.
  • Fog / Surface are background tones for sections and panels. Use to create visual layering.

Voice & Tone

  • Clear, direct, professional. No jargon unless the audience is technical.
  • Confident but not aggressive. We illuminate compliance gaps, we don't scare.
  • Action-oriented. Every headline should hint at what the user gains.

File Formats

  • Use SVG for all logo and icon usage on the web.
  • Use color-palette.css as the single source of truth for CSS custom properties.
  • Reference this guidelines document for spacing, shadows, and component specifications.
